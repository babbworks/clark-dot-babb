Clark Tools

Clarkware is an open commitment to build industrial software that serves networks, not vendors.

Clarkware is the name for every piece of software Clark builds and releases. The commitment is simple: these tools should be open source, built for real industrial environments, and designed so that any centre, node, or regional actor can use them without asking Clark for permission.

Why open source

Industrial software has historically locked operators into vendor ecosystems. A small assembly shop that adopts an MES, a quality system, or a training platform is usually adopting a dependency. When the vendor raises prices, changes direction, or disappears, the shop is stranded.

Clarkware is built on the assumption that this pattern is avoidable. The tools should be open enough that any facility can run them independently, fork them, or replace Clark entirely without losing their operational data or their workflow. The licence is commercially friendly. The architecture is extension-based. The data formats are documented.

Sovereignty first, network second

Every Clarkware tool is designed to offer complete sovereignty, independence, and privacy to its users. A facility runs its own instance, owns its own data, and owes nothing to Clark or to any network. There are zero built-in mechanisms or expectations that operators contribute data to working networks, to Clark, or to anyone else.

Network features exist for facilities that choose to participate. Data can be pooled or published based on the needs and preferences of each company — selectively, on their terms, through controls they operate. The network is an option, never a requirement.

Design principles

Operator sovereignty

Each facility's data belongs to that facility. Nothing phones home. Nothing requires an account, a subscription, or a handshake with Clark. If a company wants to run Clarkware in complete isolation, with no network connection and no external visibility, the tools work exactly the same way.

Workstation first

Every tool starts at the bench. If the person doing the work does not benefit directly, the tool is wrong. Management views, dashboards, and reports are derived from workstation-level records, never the other way around.

Evidence by default

Every action produces a record. Human actions, AI actions, and automated actions are distinguished in the log. The audit trail is append-only and cannot be silently rewritten.

Extension, not configuration

Clarkware tools are built on extension architectures. New capabilities are added as modules, not as settings screens. This means the tools can be adapted to specific industries, workflows, and equipment without forking the core.

Who Clarkware serves

Individual facilities

A single shop can adopt the IPE to replace paper travellers, disconnected firmware tools, and manual inspection logs. No network membership required. No licence fee for the open source core.

Network nodes

Facilities that join a Clark-coordinated network get cross-node visibility, shared job context, and platform-level audit aggregation on top of their local Clarkware installation.

Regional industrial actors

Workforce boards, economic development agencies, and training institutions can use Clarkware tools to coordinate training delivery, track certification pipelines, and produce evidence of industrial capacity that is legible to investors and policymakers.

Customers and auditors

Clarkware produces structured, machine-readable quality and production records. A customer's procurement team or a compliance auditor can query the system directly rather than requesting a bespoke report.
