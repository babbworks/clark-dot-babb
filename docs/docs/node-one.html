First centre plan

Training is the first revenue proof.

The first centre is framed as a hosted training and demonstration operation with a narrow scope, not a capital-intensive facility build. Clark is the instigator and coordinator; the local centre is the operating entity.

Operating shape

The plan centers on a hosted footprint, a public bench, a certification bundle, and a training curriculum that already exists in the corpus.

Commercial shape

The first value is direct training contribution. After that come discovery, onboarding, recurring support, and finally node equity if the relationship matures.

What is in the bundle

IPC credentials

The operational training spine uses the industry standards that buyers already understand.

Clark Courses

The 16-course context layer teaches the industrial setting around the bench, not just the bench itself.

Clarkware

The software layer records the work, ties evidence to the job, and produces the public record.

Learning and Working at the centre

Learning

The centre can host reporting on industrial opportunity, local dialogue with leaders, and certification coordination delivered locally with Clark assistance.

Working

The centre can support production, cooperation, and financing support for small entities that need lower-friction access to the industrial arena.

Acquisition and conversion value

Preserve assets

If a machine shop or related facility ages out, the centre model can help preserve the value of the physical assets and the operating capacity instead of letting them sit dormant.

Transaction support

Clark can help surface the opportunity, coordinate the relationships, and support a transaction or conversion when that is the right way to keep capacity in play.

Economics, in plain terms

  • Training contribution in the base case: about CAD 62,536 net.
  • One discovery engagement: about CAD 5,000.
  • One node onboarding engagement: about CAD 25,000.
  • One year of support at a mid-band: about CAD 42,000.
  • The point is not scale at launch.
  • The point is to prove the model can generate real revenue before a larger platform raise.
  • If the proof case fails, the model gets corrected.
What this unlocks

One centre can become the first repeatable operating unit.

Once a single hosted centre works, the same method can be repeated with other facilities and other towns.
