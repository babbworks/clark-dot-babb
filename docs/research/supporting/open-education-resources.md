# Open Education Resources — Reference File

**Purpose:** Tracking open-source curricula, textbooks, and educational materials relevant to Clark's training pillars (electronics, robotics, production automation). These resources validate the thesis that institutional-quality education is moving toward open, applied, artifact-credentialed models — and they may be directly usable in Clark center curricula.

## University of Michigan — Robotics Curriculum (Full Open Source)

**Source:** [github.com/michiganrobotics](https://github.com/michiganrobotics)
**Discovered:** 2026-06-14 via [Ilir Aliu tweet](https://x.com/IlirAliu_/status/2066219876302098929)
**Local clone:** `clarkinc/resources/michiganrobotics/` (all repos)

A top-10 US engineering school has open-sourced its entire robotics degree curriculum — lectures on YouTube, textbooks on GitHub, every problem set, exam, and code artifact free.

### Why This Matters for Clark

1. **Precedent:** A world-class institution has concluded that open publication of its curriculum is better than gatekeeping it. This validates Clark's model of education "priced and shaped by what the ground needs."

2. **Directly usable:** Robotics is one of Niagara Assembly's four pillar sectors. ROB 311 (How to Build Robots and Make Them Move) and the MBot platform documentation could feed directly into Clark center training — either as reference material or as the basis for adapted curricula.

3. **Pedagogical model:** Professor Jessy Grizzle's approach — starting with applied math that matters (linear algebra for computer vision, ML, robotics) rather than four semesters of prerequisites — mirrors Clark's bench philosophy: learn by standing inside a working system, not by sitting through years of theory.

4. **The math-to-electronics bridge:** ROB 101's computational linear algebra is the same mathematics that underpins signal processing, control systems, and sensor fusion — all directly relevant to electronics assembly and test. A Clark center could use this as the mathematical foundation layer for advanced electronics training.
