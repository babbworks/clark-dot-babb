The first test case

Niagara Assembly Centre

The corpus uses Niagara Assembly as the working reference because it gives Clark a grounded place to test whether a local Education and Assembly Centre can be initiated with local leaders and facilities. Clark's role is to help make that possible, not to assume ownership or day-to-day operation.

The setting

The working plan references a Hamilton host footprint and a first-phase training and demonstration area. That remains a reference model for the site, but the public claim is narrower: Clark is helping test the case for a local centre.

The move

A professional electronics bench, a learning-and-certification spine, a local production and cooperation layer, and a public record of what happened. That is the opening move, not a land grab or a campus build.

Learning and Working at the centre

Learning

Reporting, connecting, certification

The centre can host reporting on industrial opportunity, productive dialogues with local leaders, and certification and training coordination delivered locally with Clark support.

Working

Production, cooperation, financing

The centre can support useful production, regional cooperation, and financing support for small entities that need a lower-friction path into the arena.

Software

Bundled capability

Clarkware is part of the centre's working environment, not a separate pitch layered on top of the work.

Why this is credible

The model in the corpus uses conservative training economics and a small proof case: one paid discovery engagement, one node onboarding, one recurring support relationship, and a training contribution from the host node.

Illustrative proof case

  • Training contribution: about CAD 62,536 net in the base case.
  • One discovery engagement: about CAD 5,000.
  • One onboarding engagement: about CAD 25,000.
  • One recurring support relationship: about CAD 42,000 across 12 months.

What that proves

  • Clark can generate revenue from real work.
  • The first centre can support itself without a giant starting raise.
  • The system can expand only after it has something worth expanding.
  • A local anchor can later support acquisition or conversion when that is the best way to preserve capacity.
Participation ask

If you can help place the bench, staff the bench, fill the bench, or help a local facility become a centre, this is the page to start from.

The first centre needs local leadership, a host, a trainer, an early customer, and a proof surface. It does not need a grand launch.
