## 1. IPC Workforce Data & Manufacturing Technician Shortage

### The headline numbers

- **2.1 million** manufacturing jobs could go unfilled by 2030, costing the US economy **$1 trillion** in that year alone. (Deloitte & The Manufacturing Institute, 2021 study, still the canonical citation.)
  - Source: [Deloitte/MI study](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/us-manufacturing-skills-gap-could-leave-as-many-as-2-1-million-jobs-unfilled-by-2030--deloitte-and-the-manufacturing-institute-study-finds-301281967.html)

- Updated 2024 study: manufacturers will need **3.8 million new employees by 2033**; nearly half (~1.9M) may go unfilled without intervention.
  - Source: [Manufacturing Institute 2024](https://themanufacturinginstitute.org/manufacturers-need-as-many-as-3-8-million-new-employees-by-2033/)
  - Source: [Manufacturing Dive](https://www.manufacturingdive.com/news/manufacturing-labor-shortage-2033-deloitte-mi-report-2024/713133/)

- **65%** of manufacturers cite attracting and retaining talent as their primary business challenge (2024 NAM Outlook Survey).

### The "39% technician" claim — status: NOT DIRECTLY CONFIRMED

The specific claim "39% of 2M+ unfilled US manufacturing jobs by 2031 are technician roles" was **not found verbatim** in any IPC or Deloitte publication. What the data does show:
- 8 in 10 of the hardest-to-fill job titles are entry and mid-level positions requiring only a post-secondary certificate or high-school diploma (Deloitte/MI).
- Industrial machinery maintenance technicians (270K workers in 2022) are projected to grow 16% by 2032 (BLS).
- The fastest-growing production segments are semiconductor-processing technicians and electronics/electromechanical assemblers.
- **Recommendation:** Reframe as "the majority of unfilled manufacturing positions are technician-tier and below" and cite the Deloitte/MI 80% figure.

### IPC-specific workforce publications

- **"Building Electronics Better"** — IPC's April 2024 workforce whitepaper (authored by Hernandez, Plaza, Mitchell). Key findings:
  - Electronics manufacturers globally report growth constrained by inability to recruit, onboard, retain, and upskill workers.
  - Among production jobs, **general assembler and hand solderer are the most difficult to fill**.
  - Four systemic failures: no industry-driven pipeline, ineffective onboarding, no career pathways system, no rapid upskilling infrastructure.
  - IPC has created the first three DoL-approved apprenticeship programs: Electronics Assemblers, PCB Designers, PCB Fabricators.
  - Source: [IPC Workforce Whitepaper (PDF)](https://edu.ipc.org/hubfs/Workforce%20Whitepaper%20041024.pdf)
  - Source: [IPC press release](https://www.globenewswire.com/news-release/2024/04/10/2860808/0/en/IPC-Publishes-Comprehensive-Strategy-to-Address-Electronics-Industry-s-Global-Workforce-Challenge-Calls-on-Leaders-in-Government-Business-and-Education-for-Support.html)

## 3. IndustryWeek 2025 Reshoring Survey

### Core finding: workforce beats tariffs

- A **larger, stronger skilled workforce** would bring back more manufacturing than tariffs, a lower dollar, lower taxes, or fewer regulations.
- With sufficient workforce: OEMs would reshore **30% of currently offshored production**.
- With 15% tariffs on all imports from all countries: OEMs would reshore only **23%**.
- **7-percentage-point advantage** for workforce over tariffs as a reshoring lever.
- Survey conducted Feb–Apr 2025 by Reshoring Initiative and Regions Recruiting; 500+ manufacturers.
- Source: [IndustryWeek article](https://www.industryweek.com/the-economy/competitiveness/article/55302848/a-stronger-skilled-workforce-would-boost-reshoring)
- Source: [2025 Reshoring Survey Report (PDF)](https://reshorenow.org/content/pdf/2025_Reshoring_Survey_Report_Portrait-compressed.pdf)
- Source: [AMT Online summary](https://www.amtonline.org/article/2025-reshoring-priorities)

### Four priorities for reindustrialization (from survey)

1. Level the cost playing field
2. A larger skilled workforce
3. Use Total Cost of Ownership (TCO) analysis
4. Prepare for geopolitical risk

## 4. CHIPS Act Workforce Requirements

### Scale of the workforce gap

- **90,000 position shortfall** in fab operations alone by 2030 without intervention.
- **48,000 new technician and engineering jobs** needed by 2030; only 43,500 will be filled at current rates — a **4,500-worker gap** even in the best case.
- The broader US semiconductor industry will **fail to fill 67,000 technical/engineering jobs** by 2030.
- McKinsey estimate: US chip sector will be **short 59,000 workers by 2029** even if CHIPS programs deliver fully; up to **146,000 if they don't**.
  - Source: [IEEE Spectrum](https://spectrum.ieee.org/amp/chips-act-workforce-development-2660008651)
  - Source: [AMTEC semiconductor workforce report](https://www.amtec.us.com/blog/semiconductor-workforce-report)

### Micron specifics

- Micron received **$6.14 billion** under CHIPS Act for three memory fabs (Idaho and New York).
- Must invest **at least $40 million** from CHIPS funding in workforce training in local communities.
- Boise facility operational 2025; DRAM output from 2026.
- Source: [DCD News](https://www.datacenterdynamics.com/en/news/micron-to-receive-614bn-under-chips-act-to-construct-three-memory-chips-fabs-in-idaho-and-new-york/)

### CHIPS workforce funding

- **$200 million** (FY2023–FY2027) via CHIPS for America Workforce and Education Fund to NSF for microelectronics workforce development.
- Source: [Congress.gov CRS Report R47523](https://www.congress.gov/crs-product/R47523)

### Clark-relevant gap

The CHIPS workforce gap is overwhelmingly at the **technician tier** — 2-year associate degrees, certificate programs, hands-on assembly and testing skills. This is precisely the tier Clark trains for. Fab technicians need soldering, assembly, testing, and quality control skills that overlap heavily with PCBA assembly training.

## 6. Ontario College Contraction

### Headline numbers — CONFIRMED

- **$1.8 billion** in revenue losses across Ontario's 24 public colleges.
- **600+ programs** suspended or cancelled.
- **~8,000 positions** eliminated (OPSEU/SEFPO union figure; some sources say approaching 10,000).
- Source: [CBC — Nearly 10,000 job losses](https://www.cbc.ca/news/canada/toronto/ontario-college-layoffs-1.7581037)
- Source: [Colleges Ontario pre-budget submission](https://www.collegesontario.org/en/news/ontario-colleges-warn-funding-gap-threatens-workforce-training-urging-action-in-2026-budget)

### Root cause

- Federal international student caps (late 2023–2024) caused sharp enrollment declines.
- Ontario's domestic tuition freeze (in place since 2019) compounded the revenue gap.
- International students had been subsidizing ~one-third of institutional revenue.

### Electronics/manufacturing programs affected — CONFIRMED

**Fanshawe College (London, ON):**
- **Manufacturing Engineering Technician (Co-op)** — suspended effective Fall 2026.
- **Electronics and Embedded Systems Development** — suspended effective Winter 2026.
- Total: 59+ programs suspended across three rounds of cuts.
- Source: [Fanshawe program suspensions page](https://www.fanshawec.ca/admissions-finance/program-suspensions)
- Source: [Saugeen Times](https://saugeentimes.com/a-grim-outlook-for-ontario-colleges-as-fanshawe-adds-to-the-more-than-50-programs-suspended/)

**Mohawk College (Hamilton, ON):**
- **$50 million** projected budget shortfall for 2025-26.
- **200–400 jobs** cut; 16 programs suspended in December 2025.
- 62 additional full-time employees laid off by March 2026; 10 vacant positions eliminated.
- **City School** (free community education program) suspended.
- Specific manufacturing program suspensions not yet itemized in public reporting, but the scale of cuts (20% of admin staff, 16+ programs) guarantees impact on applied technology programs.
- Source: [CBC — Mohawk cuts 20% of admin](https://www.cbc.ca/news/canada/hamilton/mohwak-college-layoffs-program-cuts-1.7403872)
- Source: [CBC — 62 more laid off](https://www.cbc.ca/news/canada/hamilton/mohawk-college-layoffs-9.7145170)

### Projected deficit

- College sector faces deficit of **up to $1.5 billion by 2027-28** without intervention.

## 8. Cross-Border Manufacturing Data (US-Canada)

### Buffalo-Niagara gateway

- The Buffalo-Niagara corridor facilitates **$83–90+ billion** in annual US-Canada trade — approximately **15% of all bilateral trade** between the two countries.
- The Peace Bridge corridor alone processes **~$40 billion** in bilateral trade annually.
- **1.18 million commercial vehicle crossings** recorded in 2024.
- Source: [Buffalo Niagara Partnership](https://www.thepartnership.org/cross-border-economy/)
- Source: [Invest Buffalo Niagara](https://buffaloniagara.org/industry/logistics-and-distribution/)
- Source: [BTS TransBorder Freight 2024](https://www.bts.gov/newsroom/transborder-freight-annual-report-2024-0)

### CUSMA rules of origin for electronics

- CUSMA requires Regional Value Content (RVC) rules: products must include a specified percentage of materials/labour from Canada, US, or Mexico to qualify.
- Product-specific rules of origin (PSROs) for electronics are in Annex 4-B of the CUSMA agreement, indexed by HS code.
- Electronics assembled from North American components can qualify for duty-free treatment under CUSMA.
- Source: [EDC — CUSMA rules of origin](https://www.edc.ca/en/blog/complying-with-cusma-rules-of-origin.html)
- Source: [RSM — CUSMA rules amid tariff tensions](https://rsmus.com/insights/services/business-tax/understanding-cusma-rules-amid-us-canada-tariff-tensions.html)

### Clark implication

A Clark node in Hamilton, ON assembling PCBAs with North American-sourced components can ship finished goods across the border under CUSMA duty-free, while Asian-sourced equivalents face 25-50% tariffs. This is a structural cost advantage.

## 9. Manufacturing Workforce Age Demographics

### The "silver tsunami"

- **4.1 million Americans** turning 65 each year from 2024–2027 — unprecedented.
- **11,200 Americans per day** turning 65 as of 2024.
- Source: [Alliance AM](https://allianceam.com/legacy/surge-of-retirees-demographic-shift-reshaping-americas-workforce-and-economy)
- Source: [WorkforceGPS](https://olderworkers.workforcegps.org/resources/2025/04/30/21/30/Silver-Tsunami-Washing-Over-the-U-S-Workforce)

### Manufacturing-specific

- Average age of a US manufacturing worker: **44.7 years**.
- Average age of a **skilled trades worker: over 50 years**.
- **56% of skilled trades workers** are nearing retirement age (50–65).
- **53% of skilled trades workers** are over 45.
- Nearly **one-quarter** of the manufacturing workforce is age 55+.
- For every skilled worker entering the workforce, **five are leaving**.
- Source: [CareerExplorer](https://www.careerexplorer.com/the-skilled-trades-shortage/)
- Source: [Remarcable — Skilled trades statistics 2026](https://www.remarcable.com/blog/skilled-trades-statistics-for-2026-the-numbers-behind-the-workforce-that-builds-everything)
- Source: [Manufacturing Institute — Aging of Manufacturing Workforce](https://themanufacturinginstitute.org/research/the-aging-of-the-manufacturing-workforce/)

### Broader workforce

- Of 5 million workers who exited the US workforce since 2021, **80% were over age 55**.
- Projected deficit: **6 million workers by 2032** from combined retirements, declining birth rates, and low labor force participation.
- Source: [NJBIA study](https://njbia.org/study-warns-of-silver-tsunami-and-coming-labor-shortages/)

## 10. Tariff Impact Data 2025-2026

### Current tariff rates on Chinese electronics

- Effective tariff on most electronics from China: **35%** (after Supreme Court struck down 2025 IEEPA tariffs, rate dropped from 45% to 35% — 10% MFN + 25% Section 301).
- Semiconductors from China: **50%** (Section 301).
- Electric vehicles from China: **100%** (Section 301).
- Some products face combined effective rates exceeding **50%** when stacking MFN duties, Section 301, Section 232, and anti-dumping/countervailing duties.
- Source: [Matric Group — China tariffs guide](https://blog.matric.com/china-tariffs-on-electronics-manufacturers)
- Source: [China Briefing](https://www.china-briefing.com/news/us-china-tariff-rates-2025/)

### Reshoring impact

- Tariffs cited in **454% more reshoring cases** in 2025 vs. 2024.
- Source: [Reshoring Initiative](https://reshorenow.org/april-3-2025/)

### Canada's tariff response

- March 2025: Canada imposed **25% counter-tariffs** on broad US imports (appliances, electronics, furniture, household goods).
- September 2025: Most counter-tariffs lifted after CUSMA accommodation; **steel, aluminum, and auto tariffs remain**.
- **$1 billion Regional Tariff Response Initiative (RTRI)** since September 2025 to support affected businesses.
- May 2026: Additional **$1.5 billion** announced plus **$1 billion BDC program** and **$500M additional RTRI funding**.
- Canada's computer and electronics manufacturing **rose 3%** in first 9 months of 2025 vs. 2024 — tariffs are net positive for domestic production.
- Source: [Canada.ca tariff response](https://www.canada.ca/en/department-finance/programs/international-trade-finance-policy/canadas-response-us-tariffs.html)
- Source: [Bank of Canada analysis](https://www.bankofcanada.ca/2026/05/sparks-at-bank-article-2026-13/)

## 11. Global Electronics Production by Region

### PCB production

- Global PCB market: **$82.2 billion** in 2024, projected **$113.7 billion by 2033** (CAGR 3.8%).
- **Asia-Pacific: 85%+** of global PCB production (by some measures 67% by volume; 85% by capacity).
  - **China alone: 32.8%** of global PCB market share in 2024 (~$26.79B output value).
  - China: **40.8 billion square inches** of PCBs across ~900 fabrication facilities.
- **North America: 7.6 billion square inches** (roughly **5% of global output**).
- **Europe: ~6.3 billion square inches**.
- Source: [Dataintelo PCB/PCBA Market Report](https://dataintelo.com/report/pcb-pcba-market)
- Source: [AnyPCBA — Top 10 PCB manufacturers](https://www.anypcba.com/blogs/pcb-manufacturing-knowledge/top-10-pcb-manufacturers-globally-a-2024-deep-dive.html)
- Source: [Kingford — Global PCB center shift](https://www.kingfordpcb.com/industry-news/6011.html)

### PCBA (assembly) services

- Global PCB Assembly Services market: **$90 billion** in 2024.
- Asia-Pacific: **60%+ of global PCBA market** in 2025.
- North America's share is concentrated in high-reliability segments: aerospace, defence, medical.

### The scale of the gap Clark addresses

North America produces roughly **5% of global PCBs** while consuming a vastly larger share. The assembly gap is even wider for high-mix, small-batch work — most of it is either done in Asia with long lead times or in a handful of large domestic EMS providers. Clark targets the underserved segment: **regional, small-batch, high-mix PCBA** for defence, industrial, and medical applications.

## 12. Defence Electronics Spending

### US defence electronics

- US defence electronics market: **$22.6–52 billion** in 2024 (range depends on scope definition).
  - Narrow definition (military electronics components/systems): ~$24B in 2025, growing to $31.8B by 2030 (CAGR 5.81%).
  - Broader definition (including software, integration, services): ~$52B in 2024.
- Global defence electronics: **$178.3 billion** in 2025, growing to **$234.5 billion by 2030**.
- Source: [Mordor Intelligence](https://www.mordorintelligence.com/industry-reports/defense-electronics-market)
- Source: [Research and Markets — US military electronics](https://www.researchandmarkets.com/report/united-states-military-electronics-market)
- Source: [Precedence Research](https://www.precedenceresearch.com/defense-electronics-market)

### Canadian defence electronics & procurement

- Canada's defence budget: **$81.8 billion** in new investments pledged in Budget 2025.
- **$6.6 billion** allocated specifically to Canada's Defence Industrial Strategy.
- Defence Industrial Strategy targets **70% of defence acquisitions** awarded to Canadian firms.
- Industrial and Technological Benefits (ITB) Policy requires companies awarded major defence contracts to undertake business activity in Canada equal to the contract value.
- Source: [Canada.ca DIS](https://www.canada.ca/en/department-national-defence/corporate/reports-publications/industrial-strategy/security-sovereignty-prosperity.html)
- Source: [Osler — Canada defence M&A](https://www.osler.com/en/insights/updates/canadas-defence-build-up-new-ma-playbook/)

### ITAR and domestic sourcing

- ITAR (International Traffic in Arms Regulations) restrictions increasingly drive demand for **domestic sourcing** of defence electronics.
- Canada holds a bilateral ITAR exemption with the US, but this exemption is under scrutiny and has limits.
- Small/medium defence electronics producers report difficulty accessing capital due to ESG restrictions from lenders.
- Source: [CGAI — Canada and ITAR exemption](https://www.cgai.ca/th_pp_canada_the_eu_and_the_future_of_the_itar_exemption)
