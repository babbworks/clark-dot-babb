Project Map

This page explains how the public site, the updates feed, the evidence stack, the library, and the underlying corpus fit together. The point is to make the project readable as a system, not a pitch deck.

Public story

Home, Why Clark, and Learning & Working carry the argument. They explain the thesis, the two-pillar frame, and the first centre hypothesis.

Current work

Updates records changes and field notes. It should read like a changelog and not like an ad feed.

Proof layer

Evidence and the library keep claims, corrections, and the supporting record separated enough to stay credible.

Where the corpus lives

Library holds the hybrid public documents and the source corpus from venture, products, pilots, courses, planning, and Clark IPE development.

Project structure at a glance

Public site

Home, thesis, method, first centre, evidence, updates, map, and participation pages for outside readers.

Library

Hybrid pages that condense the nucleus of the project and expose the source corpus through one searchable index.

Source corpus

The product docs, project synthesis, working papers, pilots, and Clark IPE materials that ground the public story.
